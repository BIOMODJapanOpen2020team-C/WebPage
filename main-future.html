Future

We designed a chemical network capable of operating in a manner similar to the human immune system, using DNA-DNA interactions. It has been shown that the engineered response superficially mimics three basic steps of the immune response, namely the two step reaction of memorizing the virus (primary response) and mimicked antibody generation (secondary response). In addition, even though the engineered reaction network is completely synthetic, however entirely made of biological molecules (DNA), which means its perfect biocompatibility. Consequently, because DNA can regulate the cellular state, it may be possible to encode genetic functions (e.g. protein expression) to the output of the device. It is therefore possible to scale this prototype up to smart molecular nano controllers for the Adaptive Immune System. Although our current model would need to be enhanced and combined with other chemistries to achieve the purpose, its conceptual usefulness is proved through this project.
