Purpose

A fully integrated artificial adaptive immune system has not been tested. In addition, other difficulties can arise in the creation of functional molecular systems. In the present research, however, we hypothesise the feasibility of developing a network of reactions capable of operating in a manner similar to the human adaptive immune system, using DNA as simplified artificial analogues to imitate adaptive immune response. DNA provides an excellent platform for the design of reaction networks with arbitrary topologies with high programmability. Synthetic DNA-based systems have been used to explore the possibilities of imitating both simple and complex systems, building on the richness of DNA computing and manipulation. Our challenge is to develop a reaction network which produces antibodies that lock onto a part of the foreign body known as an antigen. DNA interactions result in DNA-strand-displacement since this event causes a dynamic cascade of reactions. In the following, we plan to demonstrate the step-by-step construction of a prototype, termed hereinafter as an adaptive immune response simulator(AIRS).
