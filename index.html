Abstract

Immune cells possess the ability to survey tissues and detect pathological insults and anomalies gives them a specific medium for body and disease interaction. In the event of an infection, our bodies exhibit an adaptive immune response(AIR) focussed at neutralizing the pathogen. However, due to various factors, in multiple scenarios, the human immune system(HIS) is unable to actively provide an immune response in the event of an infection. Here we show that the AIR in humans can be simulated using DNA logic gates. We found that most of the functional aspects of our immune system can be replicated using DNA circuits. It demonstrates the successful mimicking of the AIR of our immune system using DNA, due to its high programmability and robustness. Moreover, we include a long-term vision of an artificial immune system that can reliably diagnose diseases, remodel microenvironments of diseases, and treat a potentially wide range of challenging diseases by providing a “boost” to our immunity.
